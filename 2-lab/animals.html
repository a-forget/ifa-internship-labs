African bush elephant
Loxodonta africana 		Africa Forests, grasslands, woodlands, wetlands and agricultural land.

Herbivore; feeds mostly on grasses, creepers, herbs, leaves, and bark. 		It is the largest living terrestrial animal.
Asian elephant
Elephas maximus 		Asia Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia, from India in the west to Borneo in the east, and Nepal in the north to Sumatra in the south. It frequently inhabits grasslands, tropical evergreen forests, semi-evergreen forests, moist deciduous forests, dry deciduous forests and dry thorn forests. Herbivore; known to feed on at least 112 different plant species. They consume up to 330 lbs of plant matter per day and up to 200 litres of water per day.
Blanding's turtle
Emydoidea blandingii 		North America Semi-aquatic; wetlands. Omnivorous; eats crustaceans, insects, snails and other invertebrates, fish, fish eggs, frogs, carrion, berries, seeds, coontail, duckweed, sedge, and bulrush. It is capable of catching live fish. It shows few or no common signs of aging and is physically active and capable of reproduction into eight or nine decades of life.

Eurasian lynx
Lynx lynx 		Europe It is widely distributed from Northern, Central and Eastern Europe to Central Asia and Siberia, the Tibetan Plateau and the Himalayas. It inhabits temperate and boreal forests up to an elevation of 5,500 m (18,000 ft). Carnivore; small to fairly large-sized mammals and birds (European hare to wild boars to young moose). It is capable of killing deer many times its weight (~55 lbs vs. 330 lbs).
Fisher
Pekania pennanti 		North America Although fishers are competent tree climbers, they spend most of their time on the forest floor and prefer continuous forest to other habitats. They have been found in extensive conifer forests typical of the boreal forest, but are also common in mixed-hardwood and conifer forests. Fishers prefer areas with continuous overhead cover with greater than 80% coverage and avoid areas with less than 50% coverage. Fishers are more likely to be found in old-growth forests. Omnivore; primarily snowshoe hares and porcupines but have been known to supplement their diet with insects, nuts, berries, and mushrooms. Fishers are not regularly subject to predation and are one of the few predators that prey on porcupines.
Mantis shrimp
Odontodactylus scyllarus 		Africa Epipelagic seabed across the Indo-Pacific, from the Marianas to East Africa south to South Africa. Gastropods, bivalves, and crustaceans. It has the fastest punch in the world. Its punch accelerates over 10,000g, reaching speeds of 20 m/s, and delivering 340 lbf of force.
Platypus
Ornithorhynchus anatinus 		Australia Semi-aquatic; freshwaters of eastern Australia. Carnivore; insect larvae, annelid worms, shrimp, crayfish, bivavles, tadpoles, and fish eggs. Its swimming style is unqiue among mammals, propelling itself by alternative strokes of each front foot, while the webbed hind feet and tail are used for steering.
Tardigrade
Acutuncus antarcticus 		Antarctica Antarctica, South Georgia Island, and the South Sandwich Islands; it is found in terrestrial, marine and freshwater habitats, but are most commonly found in terrestrial habitats, living in mosses, lichens, grasses, algae, soil, and cyanophytan mats. Herbivore; cyanobacteria and green algae, among other things. They are some of the most resilient and adaptable animals in the world. Their anhydrobiosis and cryptobiosis abilities allow them to survive being frozen for decades.
